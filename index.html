Experiences

Center for Instructional Technology (CIT)

At CIT (department under Westfield State University that supports and trains faculty with online classes), I was tasked with the construction of interactive learning objects with an aim to promote online learning. My work here started out with the assistance of the Adobe Edge software, which made the process of programming much easier. But as I progressed, my projects relied less on the software and more towards the codes that I write in JavaScript, CSS, and HTML. I brought more functionalities and creativeness to my projects, and in turn produced more effective learning resources for students taking online courses.

Aside from learning new tools(Edge Animate, Dream Weaver) and languages(HTML, JavaScript, JQuery), I also got the opportunity to take on new challenges outside of my comfort zone. With every hour I spent on each project, an equivalent hour was used to develop my own applications that encompassed my collective understanding of online learning and web development.

Projects

Digit

Digit is a simple Android game that tests the user's response time. Having worn many digital watches as a kid, when I am often bored, I would play with the stopwatch function on my Casio F-91W. The whole idea of it was to start the timer, then try to stop the timer at exactly '00' milliseconds. So I brought that simple concept to my mobile application.

Built with Android Studio, and Java

- Github
No Escape
